## Notes

[[Druid Spellcasting\|Druid Spellcasting]] is divine magic granted by nature gods, totem spirits, or kami. Like [[Cleric Spellcasting\|Cleric Spellcasting]], it requires no spell book, but druids must use [[Magical Plants\|Magical Plants]] — mistletoe, holly, oak leaves (or local equivalents like bamboo, banyan, cedar, teak) — as material components, personally harvested with a special sickle by full or new moon light.

## Key Facts

- **Class:** [[Druid\|Druid]] (and [[Ranger\|Ranger]] from level 8, see [[Ranger Spellcasting\|Ranger Spellcasting]])
- **Spell Source:** Granted by gods or nature spirits — no spell book required
- **Material Components:** Magical plants such as mistletoe, holly, or oak leaves
- **Plant Harvesting:** Must be personally harvested with a special sickle by full moon or new moon. Improperly harvested plants → diminished spells (halve area of effect, then halve damage dice, then +4 to target's saving throw)
- **Oak Leaves:** Less magical than mistletoe or holly — spells using oak leaves are always diminished
- **Spell Levels:** 1 through 7
- **First 7th-Level Spell Slot:** Earned at druid level 14 (Supreme Druid)
- **Bonus Spell Slots ([[Wisdom\|Wisdom]] 13+):** Cumulative extra slots:
    - WIS 13+: 1 first-level slot
    - WIS 14+: another first-level slot
    - WIS 15+: 1 second-level slot (when castable)
    - WIS 16+: another second-level slot (when castable)
    - WIS 17+: 1 third-level slot (when castable)
    - WIS 18+: 1 fourth-level slot (when castable)
- **Memorisation:** Empty spell slots can be filled by memorising spells before adventuring; cast spells "empty" the slot until re-memorised
