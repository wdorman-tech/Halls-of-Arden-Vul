## Notes

The party's **marching order** is the position of each character: who is in front, the middle, and bringing up the rear. It governs who is hit by ambushes and traps, and which characters can attack with melee or missile weapons in different formations.

## Key Facts

- **Standard 10 ft dungeon corridor:** up to 3 characters abreast, possibly reduced to 2 or 1 if any wield large weapons (flails, two-handed swords)
- **Second rank:** can attack only with reach weapons ([[Spear\|Spear]], [[Pole Arm\|Pole Arm]]) or by firing missiles over a shorter ally (gnome, halfling) — see [[Battle Line\|Battle Line]]
- **Speed:** limited by slowest character
- **Recording:** miniatures, VTT, or paper diagram
- **Default order:** sensible parties hand over a default for entering dungeons, plus standard positions for opening doors, in 20 ft passages, etc.
- **Unclear position:** GM may resolve with a die roll
- **Caller (optional):** see [[Dungeon Adventure\|Dungeon Adventure]] for the optional party caller role
