## Notes

An **ethereal** creature is invisible, can pass through solid objects, and is immune to all attacks and spell effects, unless the attacker has a method to become ethereal or the spell/effect specifically affects creatures in the ethereal plane.

The same works in reverse: the ethereal creature **cannot attack or cast spells into the material plane**, although objects and creatures in the material plane are visible to the ethereal creature.

## Key Facts

- **Effects:** invisible, intangible; passes through solid matter
- **Immunity:** to all material-plane attacks and spells
- **Cannot attack:** the material plane (and vice versa)
- **Vision:** ethereal creature can see material plane normally
- **Sources:** [[Etherealness\|Etherealness]] spell, certain magic items (e.g. cloak/oil), being on the ethereal plane
- **Removal:** dispel/end of duration; some spells force a return
- **Distinct from [[Invisible\|Invisible]]:** invisibility doesn't grant intangibility
