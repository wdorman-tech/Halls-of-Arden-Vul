## Notes

A multi-classed character has two or three classes and advances in all of them at once, written as "[[Cleric\|Cleric]]/[[Thief\|Thief]]" or "[[Fighter\|Fighter]]/[[Cleric\|Cleric]]/[[Magic-User\|Magic-User]]". Multi-classing is for non-humans only; the available combinations are governed by ancestry. Experience is divided equally between classes (drop fractions), and in any situation the character may use whichever class's combat or saving throw table is best.

## Houserule

**Multi-classing is NOT used in this campaign.** Player characters in the Halls of Arden Vul campaign select a single class and advance only in that class — see the Player Doc for full campaign houserules.

## Key Facts

- **Eligibility:** Non-[[Human\|Human]] only
- **Number of Classes:** 2 or 3, depending on ancestry
- **Starting Age:** Use the highest starting age among the classes
- **Experience:** Divided equally between classes; fractions are dropped (e.g. 125 xp split between two classes = 62 each, 1 lost)
- **XP After Level Cap:** Even after reaching a level limit in a class, you still allocate XP to that class
- **Combat & Saves:** Choose the best table among your classes for any given situation (e.g. cleric/assassin uses cleric saves except where assassin is better)
- **Hit Points:** Roll the appropriate die for the class you're advancing in, apply CON modifier, divide by number of classes, drop fractions
- **Armour & Spells:** Cannot cast magic-user/illusionist spells in armour; cannot cast druid spells in armour a druid can't use; cannot use thief skills in armour a thief can't wear (except [[Read Languages\|Read Languages]])
