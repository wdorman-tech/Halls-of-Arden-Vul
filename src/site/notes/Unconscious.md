## Notes

A creature is **unconscious** when it is knocked out by a spell, or — much more commonly — when its [[Hit Points\|hp]] are reduced to 0 or below in combat. An unconscious creature **cannot take any actions** and can be attacked and killed automatically as if [[Magically Sleeping\|magically sleeping]].

## Key Facts

- **Effects:** no actions; attacked and killed automatically (as [[Magically Sleeping\|Magically Sleeping]])
- **From hp loss:** triggered at 0 hp; bleeding 1 hp/round to -10 hp
- **Coma after waking:** 1d6 turns; 1 week minimum rest before strenuous activity
- **At -6 hp or below:** lasting scars likely
- **Damage threshold for instant kill:** any extra damage on an unconscious creature (besides bleeding) instantly kills
- **From [[Subdual Damage\|Subdual Damage]]:** 0 hp from subdual = unconscious but not dying
- **From [[Damage\|Temporary Damage]]:** 0 hp from temporary = unconscious until recovery
- **Removal:** healing to 1+ hp; [[Heal\|Heal]]; time
- **Stacks with:** functionally treated like [[Magically Sleeping\|Magically Sleeping]] for attacks
