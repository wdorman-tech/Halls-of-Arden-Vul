## Notes

Often, discretion is the better part of valour. A character in melee combat who runs away is **vulnerable to a free attack** from each opponent.

If a character in melee combat flees, opponents may make an immediate attack at **+4 to hit**, even if they have already attacked. After the fleeing attack, the character is out of combat (if they survive) and can move freely.

[[Parrying\|Parrying]] may be used against a fleeing attack.

If you are not in melee combat range of anyone (no enemy within 10 ft), running isn't "fleeing from combat" — it's just movement.

## Key Facts

- **Fleeing attack:** +4 to hit, even if attacker already attacked
- **Parry allowed:** [[Parrying\|Parrying]] applies as normal
- **Out of melee range:** not "fleeing" — just a normal move action
- **Exception — blocked:** if the opponent can't reach you (e.g. you're in the second rank with allies between), no fleeing attack
- **After fleeing:** character is out of combat (if they survive)
- **Pursuit rules:** see Section 1.5.3 for outdoor escape and underground pursuit
