## Notes

**Poison** is both a [[Cleric\|Cleric]] / [[Druid\|Druid]] spell and a general game mechanic.

## Spell Stats (Cleric / Druid)

- **Class:** [[Cleric\|Cleric]] / [[Druid\|Druid]]
- **Level:** 4th (Cleric) / 4th (Druid)
- **School:** [[Divine\|Divine]] [[Transmutation\|Transmutation]] / [[Druidic\|Druidic]] [[Transmutation\|Transmutation]]
- **Range:** Touch
- **Duration:** Instantaneous
- **Area of Effect:** A 1ft cube for every 2 levels you have in the caster's class, including no more than one creature
- **Components:** V, S
- **Casting Time:** 7 segments (Cleric) / 5 segments (Druid)
- **Saving Throw:** Depends on use of spell (see below)

## Spell Details

When you complete the words and gestures of this spell, your touch becomes deadly poison. An alternative use of the spell is to make a consumable substance poisonous. In both cases you must touch the target.

If your target is a creature, then when you cast this spell, it can choose to attempt a [[Saving Throw\|saving throw]] vs poison. If it passes the saving throw, it isn't affected. Creatures that are immune to poison aren't affected by this spell. Gods, demigods, artifacts, relics, and other things of equivalent power aren't affected.

- If your target is a creature and it fails its saving throw, it dies.

- If your target is an edible or potable, it becomes lethally poisonous. The first creature to eat or drink from it must attempt a saving throw vs poison or die, unless that creature is immune to poison. When a creature passes or fails this saving throw, the remaining poison is nullified.

## General Game Mechanic

Poisons include animal venoms (snake bites, spider bites), vegetable toxins (often used on poisoned needles or trap mechanisms), and magical poisons such as clouds of poisonous gas. When poison enters the bloodstream (ingestion, inhalation, wound), the character must roll a **[[Saving Throw\|Saving Throw]] vs. poison**.

If the save is **failed**, the character **dies** (instantly in game terms, though typically incapacitated immediately with brief reversibility). The save may have a modifier ranging from **+2** for weak toxins (such as a Large Spider's) to **-4** for particularly lethal poisons.

Death is reversible if **[[Slow Poison\|Slow Poison]]** or **[[Neutralise Poison\|Neutralise Poison]]** is cast soon enough (the time window depends on the cleric's level). After full death, the poison must be **flushed from the bloodstream** before [[Raise Dead\|Raise Dead]] or [[Resurrection\|Resurrection]] can succeed.

Not all poisons are lethal — many inflict **poison damage** instead, often with a save for half damage.

### Key Facts

- **Roll:** save vs. poison
- **Failure:** death (most poisons) or damage (some)
- **Modifiers:** +2 (weak) to -4 (lethal)
- **Reversal:** [[Slow Poison\|Slow Poison]] or [[Neutralise Poison\|Neutralise Poison]] in time
- **Players using poison:** strongly discouraged; many GMs limit it
- **Paladins:** may not use poison
- **Assassins:** may craft vegetable-based toxins
- **Venom degradation:** harvested venom denatures within hours
- **Large creatures:** dragons and other large creatures often immune to standard poisons
- **Disease-equivalent:** see [[Disease\|Disease]] for similar mechanics
- **Insanity-equivalent:** see [[Insanity\|Insanity]]

## Reversed Form (Spell)

The Poison spell is the reverse of [[Neutralise Poison\|Neutralise Poison]].
