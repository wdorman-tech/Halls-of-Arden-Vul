## Notes

**Diseases** in OSRIC commonly come from monsters such as [[Mummy\|mummies]] or giant rats, with their effects detailed in the relevant creature text. Two other situations also create disease risk:

**Plagues** (such as the medieval Black Death) may sweep across the countryside. A character exposed must save vs. poison. On success, future saves vs. the same disease have +4. On failure, after **2d8 days incubation**, they suffer **-1d6 to all characteristics and die rolls** for **2d8 more days**. **If either die shows an "8"**, the character dies at the end. Otherwise, they recover at 1 point per day.

**Infections** can result from open wounds + high-risk environments (e.g. sewers). The GM may require a save vs. poison; failure leads to plague-like effects but with **hours** rather than days as the onset.

## Key Facts

- **Roll:** save vs. poison
- **Plague incubation:** 2d8 days
- **Plague duration:** 2d8 days at -1d6 penalty
- **Death trigger:** either die shows an "8"
- **Recovery:** 1 point per day after the symptomatic period
- **Cured by:** [[Cure Disease\|Cure Disease]] spell
- **Future saves:** +4 vs. same disease (whether or not survived)
