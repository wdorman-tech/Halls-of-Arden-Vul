## Notes

There are three types of **unarmed combat** in OSRIC: **Brawling**, **[[Grappling\|Grappling]]**, and **[[Overbearing\|Overbearing]]**.

**Brawling** is a normal attack roll for low damage (1d2 — fist, foot, or dagger pommel). All characters are automatically proficient with brawling — no proficiency slot needed.

**Grappling and Overbearing** use a separate sequence: declaration → fend-off attempt → unarmed to-hit roll → results table. Used when the goal isn't to kill — capturing alive, silencing a guard, subduing a charmed ally, or using superior numbers to bring down a stronger foe.

## Key Facts

**Brawling:**
- Normal attack roll vs. AC
- Damage 1d2; everyone proficient

**Grappling/Overbearing sequence:**
1. Attacker declares grapple or overbear
2. Defender (and adjacent allies) may "fend off" with a free to-hit roll if they have a weapon — success blocks the attack
3. If not fended off, attacker rolls Unarmed To-Hit Roll on Table 1.6.12a
4. On a hit, roll on the appropriate Results table (Overbearing or Grappling)
5. Overbearing successes may trigger immediate follow-up moves (melee, brawl, or grapple result without re-rolling to-hit)

**Unarmed To-Hit Roll basics:**
- Target number depends on attacker's *armour type only* (no DEX or magic)
- Most monsters use AC 2 (unarmoured) regardless of their natural AC
- A natural 1 is always a failure
- Modifiers: DEX (15+ = +1, 19+ = +2), movement rate (faster = bigger bonus), defender's armour, defender's movement, conditions

**Special:**
- **Grappling requires both hands free**
- **Overbearing** can be done with weapon and shield in hand
- **Many-legged creatures** (e.g. horses) cannot be grappled or grapple back, but can initiate overbearing
- **Non-living creatures** (undead, constructs) are immune to temporary damage (most unarmed damage)
- **Subdual damage** is NOT used for unarmed combat — use [[Damage\|Temporary Damage]] instead
