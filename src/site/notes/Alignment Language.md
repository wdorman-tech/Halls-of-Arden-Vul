## Notes

All creatures capable of speech know the **alignment tongue** of their alignment — a mode of communication built from gestures, facial expressions, and code words. Two same-aligned creatures who share no other language can still communicate reasonably well via their alignment tongue. There is one tongue per alignment (nine in total, one per cell of the alignment grid).

No one can learn the alignment tongue of an alignment they do not hold — with the sole exception of the [[Assassin\|Assassin]] class, whose ability to mimic alignment tongues is one of their core deceptions.

If a character changes alignment, they lose their old alignment tongue and gain the new one.

## Key Facts

- **Nine tongues:** one for each of the nine alignments
- **Universal among speakers:** any creature that can speak knows its tongue
- **Cannot be learned by outsiders** (except [[Assassin\|Assassin]]s)
- **Built from:** gestures, facial expressions, code words
- **Lost on alignment change:** swap your tongue when your alignment shifts
- **Lawful Good tongue, Chaotic Neutral tongue, etc.** — each cell of the alignment grid has its own
