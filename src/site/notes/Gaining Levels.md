## Notes

When a character has accumulated enough [[Experience Points\|Experience Points]] to reach the next [[Level\|Level]] on their class table, they may **gain that level** — but only after a period of training.

Training requires **1d3 weeks** to complete and costs **1,500 gp times the character's current level** (e.g., 1,500 gp to reach level 2, 3,000 gp to reach level 3, etc.). The 1d3 weeks of time matters mainly when adventures are time-critical; otherwise the only real cost is the room and board.

When the character actually levels up, they roll their [[Hit Die\|Hit Die]] (with [[Constitution\|Constitution]] adjustment) and add the result to their [[Hit Points\|hit points]]. Most classes also gain better attack bonuses, saving throws, and (for casters) new spell slots.

## Key Facts

- **Cost:** 1,500 gp x current level
- **Time:** 1d3 weeks
- **Prerequisite:** sufficient XP for the new level
- **Hit point gain:** roll new [[Hit Die\|Hit Die]] + [[Constitution\|Constitution]] adjustment (always at least +1 hp)
- **Post-cap levels:** most classes gain a fixed +1 to +3 hp instead of rolling, with no CON modifier
