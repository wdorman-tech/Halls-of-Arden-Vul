## Notes

When a character has accumulated enough [[Experience Points\|Experience Points]] to reach the next [[Level\|Level]] on their class table, they may **gain that level**. The base [[OSRIC\|OSRIC]] rule requires a period of training before the level-up actually takes effect.

Under base OSRIC, training requires **1d3 weeks** to complete and costs **1,500 gp times the character's current level** (e.g., 1,500 gp to reach level 2, 3,000 gp to reach level 3, etc.).

When the character actually levels up, they roll their [[Hit Die\|Hit Die]] (with [[Constitution\|Constitution]] adjustment) and add the result to their [[Hit Points\|hit points]]. Most classes also gain better attack bonuses, saving throws, and (for casters) new spell slots.

> [!warning] Houserule: training cost & training time are removed in this campaign
> The base-game **1d3 weeks of training** and **1,500 gp × current level training cost** are both **removed** by the [[Modified XP and Leveling\|Modified XP and Leveling]] houserule. In the Halls of Arden Vul, the moment a character has the XP for their next level **and** rests in a safe location (out of the dungeon, or at minimum at a friendly faction's base), they level up immediately — no gold cost, no waiting period.
>
> Gold can still be spent on optional [[Downtime\|Downtime]] activities (including [[Training (Downtime)\|Training (Downtime)]] for an XP boost, capped at 1,000 gp per week at 1 XP per gp), but **none of that is required to level up.**

## Key Facts

- **Cost (base OSRIC):** 1,500 gp × current level — **removed by [[Modified XP and Leveling\|Modified XP and Leveling]]**
- **Time (base OSRIC):** 1d3 weeks — **removed by [[Modified XP and Leveling\|Modified XP and Leveling]]**
- **Houserule trigger:** sufficient XP + safe-location rest → level up immediately
- **Hit point gain:** roll new [[Hit Die\|Hit Die]] + [[Constitution\|Constitution]] adjustment (always at least +1 hp)
- **Post-cap levels:** most classes gain a fixed +1 to +3 hp instead of rolling, with no CON modifier
