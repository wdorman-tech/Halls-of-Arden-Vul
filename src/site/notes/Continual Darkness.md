## Spell Stats

- **Class:** [[Cleric\|Cleric]] / [[Illusionist\|Illusionist]]
- **Level:** 3rd
- **School:** [[Divine Transmutation\|Divine Transmutation]] / [[Phantasmal Transmutation\|Phantasmal Transmutation]]
- **Range:** 120ft (Cleric) / 60ft (Illusionist)
- **Duration:** Permanent
- **Area of Effect:** Globe of 60ft radius (Cleric) / 30ft radius sphere (Illusionist)
- **Components:** V, S
- **Casting Time:** 6 segments (Cleric) / 3 segments (Illusionist)
- **Saving Throw:** None (Illusionist) / See below (Cleric)

## Notes

A divine or phantasmal darkness is created, emanating from the target (a creature, object, or point in space). Light, including sunlight, does not illuminate anything within 60ft of the target.

## Details

If the target is in an area of magical light (such as the radius of a [[Light\|Light]] spell), both spells (darkness and light) are cancelled.

The darkness is stationary, unless cast on a creature or object that moves, in which case it moves with that creature or object.

The caster can choose to target a creature's eyes or other sense organs. If it fails its [[Saving Throw\|saving throw]], or chooses not to attempt one, then the creature is [[Blinded\|Blinded]] for the spell's duration. The target creature applies its Dexterity save modifier, if it has one. If it passes, then your spell produces a stationary point of darkness in the air just behind the target creature, rather than affecting the creature itself.

This spell has the same limitations on concurrent castings and chance of ending as [[Continual Light\|Continual Light]] (max 3 active, 1-in-12 monthly chance to end).

As an optional rule, some GMs rule that infravision can see through the divine version of darkness spells. This optional rule only affects darkness spells cast using a cleric spell slot.

## Reversed Form

This is the reverse of [[Continual Light\|Continual Light]] (cleric and illusionist).
