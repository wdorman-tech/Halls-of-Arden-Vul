## Notes

A **battle line** is a tighter formation than the [[Spacing\|basic spacing rules]] permit. Small or Medium creatures actually only need **3x3 ft** of space to fight, move, and cast spells — they don't need the whole 5 ft square. In a space at least **9 ft wide** (and most relevantly, **10 ft wide dungeon corridors**), characters can form a closer line.

A battle line can move and fight normally, and it's the smartest way to move through a dungeon when corridors are wide enough.

## Key Facts

- **Minimum width:** 9 ft (or 10 ft, the dungeon standard)
- **Per character:** 3 ft of side-to-side space minimum
- **5 ft corridor:** single file only
- **Weapons:** some weapons require more side-to-side space (battle axes 4 ft) and may not fit
- **Three spear-wielders** fit in a 10 ft line; **three battle-axe wielders** do not
- **Front rank:** can be attacked by the enemy front rank
- **Second rank:** completely protected from melee unless reach weapons are present, but can attack with reach weapons (spears, pole arms) over the front rank's head
- **Second rank with reach weapons:** can attack the enemy front rank
- **Fleeing through ranks:** a fleeing back rank is protected from fleeing-attacks if the front rank stays in place
