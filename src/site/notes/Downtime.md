## Notes

Now that gold no longer auto-converts to XP and training is no longer mandatory (see [[Modified XP and Leveling\|Modified XP and Leveling]]), the campaign provides four standard **downtime activities** that PCs can do between adventures to convert gold into XP, reputation, healing, or trouble.

This is still a sandbox — if you have a different plan, propose it.

## The Rule

| Activity | Cost | Benefit |
|---|---|---|
| [[Training (Downtime)\|Training (Downtime)]] | Up to 1,000 GP/week | 1 XP per GP. Must train with an established willing NPC. |
| [[Connecting (Downtime)\|Connecting (Downtime)]] | Up to 1,000 GP/week | 0.5 XP per GP. Reputation in town improves the more you Connect. Good = charity, neutral = political networking, etc. |
| [[Carousing\|Carousing]] | Up to 500 GP/week | 1 XP per GP. **Roll on the Carousing Effects table.** |
| [[Recuperating (Downtime)\|Recuperating (Downtime)]] | Up to 500 GP/week | 1 XP per GP. All lasting effects (poison, injuries, lost HP) recover at **double rate**. |

## Why this matters

- Gold once again has meaningful uses — paying for training, networking, partying, or recovery.
- Different activities have different XP-to-GP ratios, plus side effects (reputation, healing, random events).
- Carousing is the dramatic engine: it generates rumours, allies, enemies, marriages, and disease.
