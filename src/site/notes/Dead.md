## Notes

A **dead** character can only be revived with [[Raise Dead\|Raise Dead]], [[Resurrection\|Resurrection]], or [[Reincarnation\|Reincarnation]]. Some ancestries cannot be raised or resurrected.

There is one notable **exception**: fatal poisons can be reversed with [[Slow Poison\|Slow Poison]], [[Neutralise Poison\|Neutralise Poison]], or antidotes within a time window determined by the cleric's level or the antidote's potency. So death from poison may be reversible if the spell or antidote is used quickly enough.

## Key Facts

- **Effects:** no actions of any kind; awaiting raise/res or final death
- **Duration:** permanent unless raised
- **Removal:** [[Raise Dead\|Raise Dead]], [[Resurrection\|Resurrection]], [[Reincarnation\|Reincarnation]]
- **Resurrection Success roll required:** see [[Resurrection Success\|Resurrection Success]] — failure means the character can never be raised again
- **Ancestry exclusions:** [[Elf\|Elf]] and [[Half-Orc\|Half-Orc]] have spirits, not souls — cannot be raised
- **Poison exception:** time-windowed reversal via [[Slow Poison\|Slow Poison]] / [[Neutralise Poison\|Neutralise Poison]] / antidotes
- **Stacks with:** N/A (final state)
- **Trigger:** -10 hp; massive failed save (death effects); poison death; [[Life Energy Levels\|level drained]] below 1
