## Notes

The **paralyzed** condition most commonly arises from [[Hold Person\|Hold Person]] or [[Hold Monster\|Hold Monster]] spells, although other situations can cause complete paralysis. Held creatures are **physically helpless**.

If you're holding a hand weapon and are not in melee range of another enemy, you can use your weapon to **automatically hit and kill** the held creature, provided your weapon can affect it.

When you want to make a normal attack against a held creature **but another enemy is in melee range of you**: weapon attacks, unarmed attacks, or touch spells **always hit** the held target (unless you choose to miss). On a hit, you may choose to inflict **maximum damage** instead of rolling. **If every creature you're attacking is held, magically sleeping, or unconscious, your number of attacks per round is doubled.**

## Key Facts

- **Effects:** physically helpless; no actions
- **No threat in melee range:** automatic kill (if weapon can affect)
- **Threat in melee range:** automatic hit, max damage option, double attacks vs. all-helpless targets
- **Saving throws:** category "Paralysis" (often grouped with poison)
- **Duration:** spell-dependent — until [[Dispel Magic\|Dispel Magic]], end of effect, or save (some sources allow recurring saves)
- **Removal:** [[Dispel Magic\|Dispel Magic]], [[Remove Paralysis\|Remove Paralysis]], [[Heal\|Heal]], end of effect
- **Stacks with:** any other condition
- **Common sources:** [[Hold Person\|Hold Person]], [[Hold Monster\|Hold Monster]], [[Ghoul\|Ghoul]] paralysis, [[Carrion Crawler\|Carrion Crawler]], certain monster touches, traps
- **Cure Light Wounds:** does NOT remove paralysis
