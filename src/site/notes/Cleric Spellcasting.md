## Notes

[[Cleric Spellcasting\|Cleric Spellcasting]] is the divine magic granted to a [[Cleric\|Cleric]] by their patron deity or pantheon. Unlike a [[Magic-User\|Magic-User]], a cleric needs no spell book — the god directly grants spells from the cleric's available list, though the deity may substitute or withhold spells based on the cleric's standing. The cleric must touch their [[Holy Symbol\|Holy Symbol]] to cast most spells.

## Key Facts

- **Class:** [[Cleric\|Cleric]] (and [[Paladin\|Paladin]] from level 9, see [[Paladin Spellcasting\|Paladin Spellcasting]])
- **Spell Source:** Granted directly by deity or pantheon — no spell book required
- **Material Component:** [[Holy Symbol\|Holy Symbol]] required for most spells
- **Spell Levels:** 1 through 7
- **First 7th-Level Spell Slot:** Earned at cleric level 16
- **Bonus Spell Slots ([[Wisdom\|Wisdom]] 13+):** Cumulative extra slots:
    - WIS 13+: 1 first-level slot
    - WIS 14+: another first-level slot
    - WIS 15+: 1 second-level slot (when castable)
    - WIS 16+: another second-level slot (when castable)
    - WIS 17+: 1 third-level slot (when castable)
    - WIS 18+: 1 fourth-level slot (when castable)
- **Memorisation:** Empty spell slots can be filled by memorising spells before adventuring; cast spells "empty" the slot until re-memorised
- **Slot Mechanics:** A first-level spell can only go in a first-level slot; can memorise the same spell in multiple slots; can choose to forget a memorised spell to free the slot
