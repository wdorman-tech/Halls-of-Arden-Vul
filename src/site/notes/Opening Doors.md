## Notes

Most doors open easily — the player states the action and the door opens. Some doors are stuck, locked, braced, or magically held (e.g. by [[Wizard Lock\|Wizard Lock]]).

## Key Facts

**Stuck Doors:**
- **Force open:** 1 round per attempt; chance from [[Strength\|Strength]] table
- **Multiple characters:** can attempt simultaneously on wide doors (each rolls)
- **Failed attempt:** still allows further tries; alerts anyone on the other side (no surprise on subsequent attempts)
- **Noisy:** may increase wandering monster checks

**Locked Doors:**
- Require a key, [[Knock\|Knock]] spell, exceptional [[Strength\|Strength]], lock-picking ([[Thief\|Thief]] or [[Assassin\|Assassin]]), or breaking down

**Picking Locks:**
- 1 round to 1 turn per attempt (typically 1d4 rounds)
- Thief/Assassin skill — see Section 1.3.10.2

**Chopping Down Doors:**
- A standard door takes at least 1 turn
- Wandering monster check should be made (often higher than normal)
- No surprise on creatures on the other side

**Spiking Doors:**
- Iron spikes wedge a door open or shut
- About 20-30% chance the spike slips (GM discretion)
- Standard tactic for adventurers
- Note: monsters open doors automatically unless prevented
