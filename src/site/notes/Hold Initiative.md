## Notes

**Holding initiative** isn't a separately declared action — it's what you say in your [[Combat Segment\|initiative segment]] when you don't want to take your declared action *yet*. You're going to do something (you declared what at the start of the round), but you want it to take effect after the other side has acted, or after a specific event triggers.

**Important:** all decisions about *what* you'll do are made during action declarations. Hold Initiative only governs *when* the action happens — you cannot use it to change your mind based on enemy action, only to make sure the effect happens at the right moment (attacking a spellcaster only once they begin casting, opening a pit trap only after the enemy steps on it, etc.).

## Key Facts

- **Must act by segment 6**, or the action is lost
- **Cannot change action**, only timing
- **Useful for:** triggering pit traps, interrupting spellcasters, attacking only if charged, etc.
- **Segments 7-10:** reserved for after-effects; if you hold past 6 you lose your action
