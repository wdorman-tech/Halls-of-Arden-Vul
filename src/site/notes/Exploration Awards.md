## Notes

Since gold no longer awards XP (see [[Modified XP and Leveling\|Modified XP and Leveling]]), the campaign uses **exploration awards** — XP for completing **[[Tasks (XP)\|Tasks]]** of varying difficulty.

## The Rule

**TXP (Total XP).** Before each session, calculate **TXP** = the sum of XP each player needs to advance from the **start** of their current level to the next. Example: a level 1 fighter (1500), thief (1250), cleric (1500), and magic-user (2900) = TXP **7,150**, regardless of where each character actually sits within their level. TXP is recalculated only at the start of each session, not when someone levels mid-session.

**Tasks award % of TXP** to all players, divided by number of players. Henchmen get a half share, hirelings a quarter share — but they do not increase TXP.

| Task tier | Description | Reward |
|---|---|---|
| **Minor** | Smart thinking and planning — disabling a trap without injury, finding a well-hidden stash | **2% of TXP** |
| **Major** | Rarer / harder challenges, ~1-2 per "season" — solving a difficult puzzle, completing a faction quest | **5% of TXP** |
| **Extraordinary** | Very difficult, multi-session goals or major status-quo shifts — taking down a faction, locating a long-sought artifact | **10% of TXP** |

XP for Task completion is awarded **as soon as the Task is completed** (subject to change if it slows the table down).

## Why this matters

- Players are paid for **exploration and creativity**, not just combat.
- TXP scales rewards as the party levels — the system stays meaningful at every level.
- The three tiers give the GM dial-able rewards: small for cleverness, medium for hard wins, large for campaign-defining achievements.
