## Notes

**Spacing** rules govern how figures are placed in combat: who can reach whom, how many attackers can target one defender, and how creatures fit into corridors and rooms. The GM is the final referee. Outside combat, exact positioning generally doesn't matter (though it's worth tracking for traps and ambushes).

## Key Facts

**Basic spacing rules:**
- Each character fits in a 5 ft square (Small/Medium)
- "Space required" for weapons (side-to-side) is ignored — most weapons work in 5 ft
- Any weapon attacks into an adjacent 5 ft square
- A [[Reach Weapon\|Reach Weapon]] (spear, etc.) attacks "through" the rank in front or across an empty 5 ft square to the next square
- You **cannot** attack through a 5 ft square occupied by an enemy, even with a reach weapon
- You **can** attack through a 5 ft square occupied by an ally with a reach weapon
- Moving outside 10 ft of an enemy who can attack triggers a potential attack (see [[Engaged in Melee Combat\|Engaged in Melee Combat]])
- Moving into melee range ends your movement (you've closed into combat)

**Battle line (advanced spacing):**
- Small/Medium creatures only need 3 ft of side-to-side space
- In 9 ft+ wide spaces, you can form a tighter line
- 5 ft wide corridor = single file
- Some weapons (e.g. battle axes need 4 ft) can't fit in tight lines

**Creature size and space requirements:**
| Size | Space | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Tiny | <5 ft (half-square token) | Up to 8/10/12 can attack a Small/Medium/Large in a round |
| Small / Medium | 5x5 ft | Battle line in 9 ft+ |
| Large | 10x10 ft (4 squares) | Can move through 5 ft wide but can't fight there without penalty |
| Huge | 15x15 ft (9 squares) | Movement restrictions GM-adjudicated |
| Gigantic | 20x20 ft (16 squares) | Outdoor only typically |
