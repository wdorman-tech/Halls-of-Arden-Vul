## Notes

A **combat round** represents **one minute** of in-game time, subdivided into **ten six-second [[Combat Segment\|segments]]**. A round is the basic unit of combat resolution: declarations, [[Initiative\|initiative]], movement, and attacks all happen within one round, then the cycle repeats.

For comparison: outside combat, time is measured in **turns** (10 minutes each), and exploration uses turns rather than rounds.

## Key Facts

- **Length:** 1 minute (10 segments of 6 seconds each)
- **Turn:** 10 rounds = 1 turn (10 minutes)
- **Hour:** 6 turns = 1 hour
- **In a round you can usually:** move OR cast a spell OR attack (some classes get multiple attacks per round)
- **Movement:** 1/10 of round movement per segment if segment-by-segment is being tracked
- **Order:** see [[Order of Events (Combat)\|Order of Events (Combat)]]
