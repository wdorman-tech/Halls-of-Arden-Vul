## Spell Stats

- **Class:** [[Druid\|Druid]] / [[Magic-User\|Magic-User]]
- **Level:** 6th (Druid) / 5th (Magic-User)
- **School:** [[Druidic Enchantment\|Druidic Enchantment]] / [[Arcane Enchantment\|Arcane Enchantment]]
- **Range:** 40ft (Druid) / 10ft per level (Magic-User)
- **Duration:** Permanent
- **Area of Effect:** One creature
- **Components:** V, S (Druid) / V, S, M (Magic-User)
- **Casting Time:** 8 segments (Druid) / 5 segments (Magic-User)
- **Saving Throw:** Yes (see below)

## Notes

The insidious feeblemind spell affects only those who can cast spells. If the victim fails the [[Saving Throw\|Saving Throw]], they are reduced to the intellect of a child until the spell is removed by a [[Heal\|Heal]], [[Wish\|Wish]], or [[Restoration\|Restoration]] spell.

## Details

The different types of spell casting ability are not equally vulnerable to the feeblemind spell.

- Clerics and users of divine magic gain a bonus of +1 to saving throws.
- Druids and users of druidic magic have a -1 penalty to the saving throw.
- Magic-users and illusionists (and users of arcane or phantasmal magic) have a -4 penalty to the saving throw.
- Those who can use more than one type of magic must save using the average of their modifiers.
