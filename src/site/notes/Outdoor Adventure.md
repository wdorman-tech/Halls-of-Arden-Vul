## Notes

For the purposes of these rules, "**Wilderness**" means any adventure in open country, including adventures at sea. The basic unit of time for wilderness exploration is **the day**.

When starting out, each day should be resolved separately. More experienced GMs sometimes run days together.

## Key Facts

**Order of play per day:**
1. **Setup:** GM advises weather; party decides direction
2. **Navigation:** GM checks (in secret) for the party becoming lost
3. **Wandering Monster check:** GM rolls
4. **Move and Act:** party members move and/or take stationary actions
5. **Encounter:** any encounter is resolved
6. **Camp:** GM presents campsite options; party camps
7. **Wandering Monster check (camp):** GM rolls again

**Time scale:**
- Most static actions take negligible time and combine with normal moves
- See [[Outdoor Movement\|Outdoor Movement]] for hiking distances
- See [[Forced March\|Forced March]] for pushing harder

**Combat outdoors:**
- Encounter distance in *yards* not feet (1d4+4 x 10 yards)
- GM may multiply movement rates (and missile ranges) by 3 for realism
- Same combat rules otherwise apply
