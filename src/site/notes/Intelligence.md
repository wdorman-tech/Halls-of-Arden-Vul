## Notes

Intelligence is your character's ability to deduce, calculate, recall facts, and solve abstract problems. It is the prime requisite for [[Magic-User\|Magic-User]]s and [[Illusionist\|Illusionist]]s and determines how many additional languages your character can learn. A high-Intelligence character might be a scholarly mage, a clever elf, a dwarf mason who carves stone puzzles, or a multilingual barbarian with a sharp eye for detail.

Crucially, in [[OSRIC\|OSRIC]], Intelligence does **not** substitute for player skill — puzzles are solved by player thinking. A high score might earn you a hint from the GM, but it does not auto-solve anything.

## Key Facts

- **Range:** 3-18 (19+ possible by magical means)
- **Generation:** 3d6 in order, with one swap — see [[Modified Character Creation\|Modified Character Creation]]
- **Prime requisite for:** [[Magic-User\|Magic-User]], [[Illusionist\|Illusionist]]
- **Maximum additional languages:**
  - 3-7: 0
  - 8-9: 1
  - 10-11: 2
  - 12-13: 3
  - 14-15: 4
  - 16: 5
  - 17: 6
  - 18: 7
  - 19: 8
- **Illusion immunity at high INT:**
  - 19+: immune to 1st-level illusion spells
  - 20+: also immune to 2nd-level illusion spells
- **Player skill rule:** mental ability scores do not replace player decision-making or puzzle-solving. The player thinks; the character does not "make an INT check."
