## Spell Stats

- **Class:** [[Cleric\|Cleric]] / [[Magic-User\|Magic-User]]
- **Level:** 7th (Cleric) / 9th (Magic-User)
- **School:** [[Divine Conjuration\|Divine Conjuration]] / [[Arcane Conjuration\|Arcane Conjuration]]
- **Range:** 30ft
- **Duration:** Instantaneous (Cleric) / See below (Magic-User)
- **Area of Effect:** Two-dimensional rectangle 10ft wide and 20ft high (Cleric) / One summoned creature (Magic-User)
- **Components:** V, S
- **Casting Time:** 5 segments (Cleric) / 9 segments (Magic-User)
- **Saving Throw:** None

## Notes

This spell draws the attention of an extra-planar creature the caster specifies by name or type, and opens a portal at the target point through which that creature can emerge into the caster's plane of existence. The named creature is not forced to step through the gate, but something will (as determined by the GM).

## Details

If the caster knows the true name, or at least one of the true names, of a being, they can try to call on that individual being. It is still not forced to emerge. In particular, if the caster calls on a deity or being of equivalent power, it doesn't come in person, but sends a minion.

Casting this spell doesn't give the caster any control over the gated creature, and unless it's presented with a matter that obviously justifies its time and attention, it may be irascible.

The gate appears in an appropriate orientation. For example, if the caster calls on a celestial being, then the gate appears above them and the being descends. If they call on a fiend, then the gate appears below them and the being crawls up to the surface.

The GM controls the gated creature.
