## Spell Stats

- **Class:** [[Cleric\|Cleric]] / [[Magic-User\|Magic-User]]
- **Level:** 2nd (Cleric) / 3rd (Magic-User)
- **School:** [[Divine Enchantment\|Divine Enchantment]] / [[Arcane Enchantment\|Arcane Enchantment]]
- **Range:** 60ft (Cleric) / 120ft (Magic-User)
- **Duration:** 4 rounds + 1 round per cleric level / 2 rounds per MU level
- **Area of Effect:** 1, 2, or 3 creatures of caster's choice within range (Cleric) / Up to 4 creatures (Magic-User)
- **Components:** V, S, M (Cleric) / V (Magic-User)
- **Casting Time:** 5 segments (Cleric) / 3 segments (Magic-User)
- **Saving Throw:** Negates (Cleric) / Yes, see below (Magic-User)

## Notes

The selected creatures must make a [[Saving Throw\|saving throw]] against spells or be paralysed. The difficulty of the saving throw depends on the number of creatures targeted (see below).

## Details

Creatures aren't affected if they are not bipedal (human, demi-human, or humanoid-like) and size Medium or smaller.

An affected creature is allowed a saving throw vs spells. The target creature applies its Wisdom save modifier, if it has one, to this saving throw. There's a further modifier that depends on how many creatures the caster chose to affect:

**Cleric version (1, 2, or 3 creatures):**
- If chose to affect only one creature, it saves at -2
- If chose to affect two creatures, there's no modifier
- If chose to affect three creatures, each creature saves at +2

**Magic-User version (1-4 creatures):**
- If only one creature, it saves at -3
- If two creatures, they save at -1
- If three or four creatures, each makes the saving throw with no penalty

[[Held\|Held]] creatures can't move their voluntary muscles, but their hearts keep beating and they can choose to continue to breathe. If the creature has a psychic or psionic power that it can use without moving any voluntary muscles, then it can still use that power.

### Combat Effects

This is a reminder about the rules applicable to paralyzed creatures. Held creatures are physically helpless. If holding a hand weapon and not in melee, the caster can use their hand weapon to automatically kill one held creature each round. If not in melee and holding a rope, chain, or other means of binding, then instead of attacking, they can bind one held creature each round.

In some cases (if still in melee with others, have multiple attacks, or if the objective is to cast a touch spell) the caster might choose to make normal attacks rather than the automatic kill approach. If they attack a held creature with a weapon attack, unarmed attack, or touch spell, they always hit it unless they choose to miss. When they hit it with a weapon attack or unarmed attack, they can choose to inflict the maximum possible damage instead of rolling it, and if every creature being attacked is held, then their number of attacks this round is doubled.
