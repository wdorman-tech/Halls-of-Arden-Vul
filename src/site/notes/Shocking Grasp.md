## Spell Stats

- **Class:** [[Magic-User\|Magic-User]]
- **Level:** 1st
- **School:** [[Arcane Transmutation\|Arcane Transmutation]]
- **Range:** Touch
- **Duration:** 1 touch (see below)
- **Area of Effect:** One creature
- **Components:** V, S
- **Casting Time:** Fast
- **Saving Throw:** None

## Notes

This spell imbues the caster's hand with a powerful electrical charge that they may use to deliver a deadly shock. The electrical charge can be delivered either by a direct touch (requiring a successful attack roll) or through a conductive material such as metal.

## Details

The shocking grasp inflicts 1d8 hit points + 1 hit point per magic-user level of the caster, all of which is electrical [[Damage\|Damage]].

Unlike many attack spells that require a to-hit roll, shocking grasp is not lost if the caster misses their attack roll. They may try again until they successfully discharge the shock.

The caster cannot cast more spells that involve somatic or material components until they discharge the shock (which they can do at will), so they can't build up multiple shocking grasps on themselves.

The caster can't discharge the shock to an opponent when they hit the caster. They must actually hit an opponent to use the spell or use their hand to zap them through a conductive medium.

This spell is not powerful enough to deliver a dangerous shock through any significant volume of water, but a small quantity of water (a shallow puddle, for instance) could be used as a suitable conductor.
