## Notes

**Insanity** is possible as a result of certain spells or monster attacks. A character who becomes insane receives an insanity from the table below. The kind may be decided by the GM based on the cause, or rolled randomly on a d6.

## Key Facts

| d6 | Insanity |
|---|---|
| 1 | Ability Decline |
| 2 | Amnesia |
| 3 | Divergent Personality |
| 4 | Disinterest |
| 5 | Pathological Liar |
| 6 | Phobia |

- **Ability Decline:** lose 1d6 each from INT, WIS, and CHA until cured
- **Amnesia:** 50% chance per round to forget how to cast a memorised spell; each morning, 50% chance to forget a weapon proficiency for the day
- **Divergent Personality:** develops a second personality (possibly different alignment or class); each morning or stress moment, 25% chance to switch
- **Disinterest:** 25% chance at start of any combat to take no actions until actually attacked
- **Pathological Liar:** lies whenever possible until cured, no matter how outrageous
- **Phobia:** strong irrational fear of a substance/creature/situation; if forced into contact, 25% chance to become [[Confused\|Confused]] (per druidic [[Confusion\|Confusion]] spell)
- **Cure:** [[Heal\|Heal]], [[Restoration\|Restoration]], or specific cures for the cause
