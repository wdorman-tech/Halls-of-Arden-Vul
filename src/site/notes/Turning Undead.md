## Notes

[[Cleric\|Clerics]] (and [[Paladin\|paladins]] of certain levels) can "**turn**" [[Undead\|undead creatures]] and certain kinds of lesser fiends. For good and neutral clerics, this means causing them to retreat and possibly destroying them. For evil clerics, it means making them non-hostile or even bringing them under the cleric's control.

In combat, treat turning like casting a "fast" spell that takes effect in the same segment. The cleric can't attack or cast a spell in the same round. **The turning attempt cannot be interrupted** like spells can.

You roll a single **d20** when making a turning attempt.

## Key Facts

- **Roll:** d20 vs. number on the Turning Undead table by cleric level
- **Area of effect:** 180-degree arc, 100 ft range, requires holy symbol be visible to the undead
- **Walls block** the effect even for incorporeal undead
- **One attempt per group per encounter** (per day)
- **Result codes:**
  - **"--"**: no chance
  - **Number**: roll ≥ number to turn 2d6 (1d2 for fiends/paladins)
  - **"T"**: automatic turn; affects 2d6 (1d2 for fiends/paladins)
  - **"D"**: destroy 2d6 (good/neutral); evil cleric controls instead (1d2 for fiends/paladins)
  - **"D\*"**: destroy 1d6+6 (1d2 for fiends/paladins); evil cleric controls
- **Paladins as undead:** evil clerics treat paladins as Type 8 (lvl 1-2) up to Type 13 (lvl 11+); paladins cannot be destroyed by turning
- **Greater fiends:** extraplanar evil with >10 HD or >65% magic resistance can't be turned
- **Attacking turned undead:** breaks the turning; they return to normal
- **Evil cleric control roll:** d100 — 01-60 not controlled (but not hostile); 61-00 controlled; cap is the cleric's max followers
- **No XP** for undead destroyed by turning
