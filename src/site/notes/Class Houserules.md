## Notes

Houserules covering classes, alignment restrictions, and magic-user training.

## The Rule

**No alignment restrictions.** Classes no longer require specific alignments — except [[Cleric\|Cleric]]s, who must match the alignment of their chosen deity (see [[Alignment\|Alignment]]). A [[Paladin\|Paladin]] does not have to be Lawful Good, a [[Druid\|Druid]] does not have to be True Neutral, etc.

**No multiclassing.** Multiclass characters are off the table for now.

**No dual classing.** Dual classing is also off the table.

**Magic-Users get free Read Magic.** Every [[Magic-User\|Magic-User]] starts knowing the [[Read Magic\|Read Magic]] spell **in addition to** their two random and two of-choice starting spells.

**Magic-Users must pick a collegium.** Every magic-user PC must choose one of the [[Seven Collegia\|Seven Collegia]] where they learned. **All other places of arcane learning are outlawed** in the [[Archontean Empire\|Archontean Empire]] — a magic-user without a collegium of record is a criminal.

## Why this matters

- Lifting alignment restrictions enables genre-mixing characters (chaotic paladins, lawful druids, etc.) while keeping religious authenticity for clerics.
- Removing multiclassing prevents the level-cap-removed demihuman from becoming overpowered (a multiclassed unlimited fighter/thief would dominate).
- Free Read Magic removes the famous "magic-user without his one good spell" early-game pain point.
- Mandatory collegium choice ties every wizard PC to imperial politics from character creation — there are no apolitical mages.
