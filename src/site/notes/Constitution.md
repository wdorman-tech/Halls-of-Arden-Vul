## Notes

Constitution is your character's fitness, endurance, toughness, and resilience. It boosts hit points, helps you survive traumatic magical change (polymorph and similar), and makes resurrection more likely to succeed. It is also a literal limit on how many times you can be raised from the dead.

## Key Facts

- **Range:** 3-18 (19 by magical means; fighter-types receive larger HP bonuses at 17+)
- **Generation:** 3d6 in order, with one swap — see [[Modified Character Creation\|Modified Character Creation]]
- **Prime requisite for:** none (but critical for everyone, especially fighters)
- **Resurrection limit:** Your initial Constitution is the number of times you can ever be brought back from death. Each resurrection reduces it by 1 (only a [[Wish\|Wish]] can restore the score, but it never raises the cap).
- **Modifiers (condensed):**
  - 3: -2 HP, 40 resurrection, 35 system shock
  - 4: -1 HP
  - 5: -1 HP
  - 6: -1 HP
  - 7-14: no HP modifier
  - 15: +1 HP
  - 16: +2 HP
  - 17: +2 HP (+3 for Fighters/Paladins/Rangers)
  - 18: +2 HP (+4 for Fighters/Paladins/Rangers)
  - 19: +2 HP (+5 for Fighters/Paladins/Rangers); any HP die roll of 1 is treated as a 2
- **HP bonuses** apply only when you actually roll for HP at level up — once a class switches to flat HP per level, Constitution stops adding to it.
- **System shock** roll is required when forced into another body (e.g. polymorph). Fail = die.
- **Resurrection success** roll is required when raised. Fail = dead forever.
