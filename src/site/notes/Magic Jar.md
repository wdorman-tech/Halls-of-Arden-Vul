## Spell Stats

- **Class:** [[Magic-User\|Magic-User]]
- **Level:** 5th
- **School:** [[Arcane Transmutation\|Arcane Transmutation]]
- **Range:** 10ft per MU level
- **Duration:** See below
- **Area of Effect:** One creature
- **Components:** V, S, M
- **Casting Time:** 1 round
- **Saving Throw:** See below

## Notes

This risky spell allows the caster to transfer their mind into the body of another creature, trapping the other creature's soul in a gem (of not less than 100 gp value) that is the material component of the spell and becomes the magic jar.

## Details

After casting, to accomplish the transfer of souls, the caster first has to trap their own life essence in the jar prior to the transfer.

When the caster tries to take control of the other creature, it is allowed a [[Saving Throw\|Saving Throw]] (with a complicated modifier described below). If the saving throw succeeds, causing the transfer of souls to fail, the caster's soul remains trapped in the magic jar until they can take over a host or otherwise escape. Escaping is easy if the caster's own physical body is still within 10ft of the jar; the caster can return to their own body at will (it takes one round to complete the transition). If the caster's body has been moved or destroyed, the caster will probably have to take over a different body.

The caster can make successive attempts to take over the same host, but no more than one per round.

After the caster's soul moves into the jar, their physical body becomes inert and lifeless.

**Saving Throw Modifier:** Add the target's intelligence and wisdom scores together, and do the same for the caster. Subtract the caster's total from the target's. This gives a positive or a negative number (or a zero). Divide that number by 3, rounding fractions down. This is the modifier on the target's saving throw.

**Possessed Creature Escaping the Magic Jar:**

- If the difference is 4 or less in favour of the caster, or is in favour of the possessed creature, the creature can attempt a new saving throw every round.
- If the difference is from 5 to 8 in the caster's favour, one saving throw is permitted per turn.
- If the difference is 9 to 12 in the caster's favour, the saving throw is made once per day.
- If the difference is 13+ in the caster's favour, the saving throw can only be made once per week.

**Important Note:** Although the caster's escape range from the magic jar is only 10ft, a creature trapped can escape back to its own body (swapping places with the caster) with unlimited range.

While the caster's soul is in the magic jar waiting to possess another body, they can sense creatures beyond the jar, but not their nature.

The jar may be moved from its original location, but not, obviously, by the caster.

When possessing another body, the caster gains complete control of its movement and acquires any memories that the body might reflexively recall (how to fly, fight with claws, use innate magical powers, etc).

The caster does not gain any knowledge from the creature's mind, such as languages known or the location of a lair, for the mind is trapped in the magic jar.

The caster retains their own mind while possessing the other body, so if the body possesses the correct physical attributes the caster can continue to cast their spells from within the new body.
