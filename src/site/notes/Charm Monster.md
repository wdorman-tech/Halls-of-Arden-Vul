## Spell Stats

- **Class:** [[Magic-User\|Magic-User]]
- **Level:** 4th
- **School:** [[Arcane Enchantment\|Arcane Enchantment]]
- **Range:** 60ft
- **Duration:** See below
- **Area of Effect:** See below
- **Components:** V, S
- **Casting Time:** 4 segments
- **Saving Throw:** Negates effect

## Notes

This spell causes the target (or targets) to view the caster as a trusted friend, if they fail a [[Saving Throw\|Saving Throw]] vs. spells.

## Details

The spell only affects one hit die category (from the table below) per casting.

Example: if ten orcs (<1 to 1+ HD) are in the spell's range, along with a bugbear (3 to 3+ category) the caster will have to decide whether to target the orcs or the bugbear. If chose the orcs, the caster would roll 2d4. If the result of the die roll were a 4, then 4 orcs would need to make saving throws. If there are ten kobolds and an orc in range, these are all in the same hit die category (<1 to 1+). However, the weakest creature type in the category is affected first, just as the [[Sleep\|Sleep]] spell works.

If the target creature fails a saving throw, it will view the caster as a trusted friend, interpreting every word in its most positive light. [[Charmed\|Charmed]] creatures will place themselves into moderate danger, including combat, on the caster's behalf, but will not undertake risks beyond those the creature might ordinarily take. The caster must be able to talk to the charmed creature if they want to make any sort of complex "request".

Charmed creatures are allowed periodic saving throws to break the charm, based on their hit dice:

| Hit Dice of Monsters | Number Potentially Affected | Chance/Week to Break Enchantment |
|---|---|---|
| <1 to 1+ | 2d4 | 5% |
| 2 to 2+ | 1d4 | 10% |
| 3 to 3+ | 1d2 | 15% |
| 4 to 4+ | 1 | 25% |
| 5 to 5+ | 1 | 35% |
| 6 to 6+ | 1 | 45% |
| 7 to 7+ | 1 | 60% |
| 8 to 8+ | 1 | 75% |
| 9+ | 1 | 90% |

The distinction between this spell and the weaker [[Charm Person\|Charm Person]] is that this spell affects any creature, not just "persons."
