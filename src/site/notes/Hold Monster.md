## Spell Stats

- **Class:** [[Magic-User\|Magic-User]]
- **Level:** 5th
- **School:** [[Arcane Enchantment\|Arcane Enchantment]]
- **Range:** 5ft per MU level
- **Duration:** 1 round per MU level
- **Area of Effect:** 1 to 4 creatures
- **Components:** V, S, M
- **Casting Time:** 5 segments
- **Saving Throw:** See below

## Notes

A hold monster spell completely immobilises up to four creatures of any kind for the spell's duration.

## Details

The caster decides how many creatures to target with the spell:

- If only one target, the [[Saving Throw\|Saving Throw]] is made at -3.
- If two targets, each makes the saving throw at -1.
- If three or four targets, each makes its saving throw with no penalty.

If a magic item or spell operates partially to negate the effects of paralysis, failure on the saving throw will have the effect of a [[Slow\|Slow]] spell rather than completely immobilising the target.

[[Held\|Held]] creatures can't move their voluntary muscles, but their hearts keep beating and they can choose to continue to breathe.

### Combat Effects

This is a reminder about the rules applicable to paralyzed creatures. Held creatures are physically helpless. If holding a hand weapon and not in melee, the caster can use their hand weapon to automatically kill one held creature each round. If not in melee and holding a rope, chain, or other means of binding, then instead of attacking, they can bind one held creature each round.

In some cases the caster might choose to make normal attacks rather than the automatic kill approach. If they attack a held creature with a weapon attack, unarmed attack, or touch spell, they always hit it unless they choose to miss. When they hit it with a weapon attack or unarmed attack, they can choose to inflict the maximum possible damage instead of rolling it, and if every creature being attacked is held, then their number of attacks this round is doubled.
