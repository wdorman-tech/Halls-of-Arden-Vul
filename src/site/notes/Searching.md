## Notes

**Searching** for hidden treasure, traps, and secret doors is a common dungeon activity.

## Key Facts

**Secret Doors:**
- **Discovering presence:** 1 round per 10x10 ft area; **1 in 6** chance for most characters; **2 in 6** for [[Elf\|elves]] and [[Half-Elf\|half-elves]]
- **Discovering opening mechanism:** separate check (same chance) at 1 turn per attempt
- Special doors may use puzzles instead of dice
- A "thorough examination" is 1 turn per 10x10 ft area for the same purpose
- **Quick room survey** has a different scope: 1 turn per 20x20 ft area, locates open pit traps automatically and 2 in 6 chance per character to find closed pit traps; elves/half-elves get 1 in 6 chance to spot a secret door's location during this

**Traps:**
- **Stone-based:** [[Dwarf\|Dwarves]] and [[Gnome\|gnomes]] use innate stone-kenning
- **General trap detection:** [[Thief\|thieves]] and [[Assassin\|assassins]] only — limited to specific objects/small areas
- **Search time:** 1-4 rounds per object/area
- **Other characters:** cannot detect traps directly; must use 10 ft pole, throwing objects, etc.
- **Trigger rate:** untriggered/un-disarmed traps activate ~50% of the time

**Negotiation-based searching:**
- Player describes specific actions (tapping walls for hollow spots, looking for loose stones)
- GM may grant bonus, automatic success, or no effect at GM's discretion
- Off-base descriptions can *reduce* success chance
- Takes longer than standard checks; raises wandering monster odds
