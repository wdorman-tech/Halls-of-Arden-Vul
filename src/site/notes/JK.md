### 🪤 JK's SOP — Entering a Room

> [!example] 🚪 Standard procedure (verbatim)
> Before opening the door, I examine the door, the door frame, the door knob, the lock, and the floor in front of the door for any sort of triggering mechanism. I examine the ceiling, walls, floor and door for any sort of trap such as: falling hammers or boulders, unknown magical devices, or any sort of projectile, etc.
>
> I now open the door slightly and look inside for any obvious threats such as any form of being, or streams of fire, etc. anything that catches my eye. I also look for other entrances and exits in the room or nearby hallway.
>
> I open the door the rest of the way.
>
> If there is a hallway, while being very careful and deliberate about where I move, and checking the floor in the space where I am about to step before stepping there, so as not to trigger any traps, I check the floors for tripwires, pressure plates, holes, or sections of the floor that look damaged or out of place. I check the walls and ceilings for any potential traps such as projectile weapons, falling boulders, falling hammers, falling snakes, hidden monsters, etc. I then check the walls, ceilings, and floors for any hidden doors, compartments, or passages.
>
> I then look into the room at the end of the hallway. While being very careful and deliberate about where I move, and checking the floor in the space where I am about to step before stepping there, so as not to trigger any traps, I check the floors for tripwires, pressure plates, holes, or sections of the floor that look damaged or out of place. I check the walls and ceilings for any potential traps such as projectile weapons, falling boulders, falling hammers, falling snakes, hidden monsters, etc. I then check the walls, ceilings, and floors for any hidden doors, compartments, or passages.
>
> If there is an object of interest in the room: I examine the object for any sort of triggering mechanisms for a trap. If the object is on a stand, pedestal, or other device, I also check the stand, pedestal, or other device for some form of triggering mechanism or danger.
