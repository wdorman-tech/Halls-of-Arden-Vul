## Notes

The [[Hierophant\|druidic hierarchy]] is the strict pyramid that constrains advancement of high-level [[Druid\|Druids]]. Only a fixed number of druids may exist at each rank above 11, and any druid wishing to claim one of these slots must defeat its current holder in a formal challenge — usually a duel, but not always.

## Key Facts

- **Class:** [[Druid\|Druid]]
- **Hierarchy Slots:**
    - 12th level (High Druid): no more than three at any one time
    - 13th level (Archdruid): no more than two at any one time
    - 14th level (Supreme Druid): only one at any one time
- **Multiple Hierarchies:** A world may contain more than one druidic hierarchy, but only one hierarchy exists in any given location
- **Earning a Slot:** If you have enough XP for a level but no slot is open, you may issue a formal challenge to a druid currently holding that level
- **Challenge:** May be a duel or another contest. If the holder declines or doesn't accept promptly, they have lost it.
- **Loser's Penalty:** Loses one level of experience; XP is set to the very minimum to qualify for the new (lower) level
- **Cascading Challenges:** This loss may itself trigger a further challenge if the lower rank is also full
