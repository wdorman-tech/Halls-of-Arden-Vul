## Notes

[[Weapon Specialisation\|Weapon Specialisation]] is an optional rule that lets a [[Fighter\|Fighter]], [[Ranger\|Ranger]], or [[Paladin\|Paladin]] take proficiency in the same weapon twice (or three times for missile weapons), gaining bonuses to hit, to damage, and to attack rate when wielding that exact weapon. Specialisation must be chosen at character creation and is keyed to a specific weapon (e.g. "longsword", not "swords").

## Key Facts

- **Available To:** [[Fighter\|Fighter]], [[Ranger\|Ranger]], [[Paladin\|Paladin]] only
- **When Chosen:** Character creation only (unless granted later by [[Wish\|Wish]] or similar)
- **Cost:** 2 proficiencies for melee weapons and crossbows; 3 proficiencies for other missile weapons
- **Specificity:** Must specialise in an exact weapon type (e.g. "short sword"), not a weapon category
- **Bonuses:** +1 to hit, +2 to damage with the specialised weapon (non-magical bonuses — won't bypass magic-only damage immunity)
- **Increased Attack Rate (Melee, levels 1-6):** 3/2 attacks per round
- **Increased Attack Rate (Melee, levels 7-12):** 2/1
- **Increased Attack Rate (Melee, levels 13+):** 5/2 (3 attacks round 1 in segments 1, 5, 10; 2 attacks round 2 in segments 1 and 10)
- **Pulled Bow RoF (Long or Short):** 2 (1-6) → 3 (7-12) → 4 (13+)
- **Light Crossbow RoF:** 1 (1-6) → 2 in odd-numbered rounds only (7-12) → 2 (13+)
- **Heavy Crossbow RoF:** 0.5 (1-6) → 1 (7-12) → 2 in odd-numbered rounds only (13+)
- **RoF vs Attack Rate:** RoF gives extra attacks within your normal initiative segment; better attack rate changes the segment in which you attack (usually 1 and 10)
- **Specialised Attack Combinations Don't Stack:** When using the specialised weapon, the specialist attack rate replaces (does not add to) your level-based [[Multiple Attacks\|Melee Attack Combination]]
- **Double Specialisation (optional):** A third proficiency may further specialise melee weapons (other than pole arms or two-handed swords); +3 to hit, +3 damage. GM may disallow.
