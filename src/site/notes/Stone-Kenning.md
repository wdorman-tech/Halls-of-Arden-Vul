## Notes

Stone-Kenning is the underground perceptiveness shared by [[Dwarf\|dwarves]] and [[Gnome\|gnomes]] (and [[Imperial Goblin\|Imperial Goblin]]s via the gnome statline). When the character closely examines stonework, the GM rolls percentile checks for various findings. Dwarves and gnomes use slightly different category lists and percentages — dwarves are better at construction details and traps; gnomes are better at slopes and have a unique direction-of-north check.

## Key Facts

- **Trigger:** examining stonework closely
- **Dwarven percentages ([[Dwarf\|Dwarf]]):**
  - Detect slopes or grades: 75%
  - Detect new construction: 75%
  - Detect sliding or shifting rooms or walls: 65%
  - Detect traps involving stonework: 50%
  - Determine depth underground: 50%
- **Gnomish percentages ([[Gnome\|Gnome]] / [[Imperial Goblin\|Imperial Goblin]]):**
  - Detect slopes or grades: 80%
  - Detect unsafe ceiling, walls, or floor: 70%
  - Determine depth underground: 60%
  - Determine direction of north underground: 50%
- **GM may modify** these chances based on conditions
