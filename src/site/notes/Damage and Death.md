## Notes

When a character or creature is hit, the [[Damage\|Damage]] is deducted from its [[Hit Points\|Hit Points]]. When hp reach **0**, the character is **unconscious** and continues to lose **1 hp per round from blood loss** until death at **-10 hp**.

Any additional damage suffered by an unconscious creature (other than the bleeding out) **kills the creature instantly**.

The bleeding may be stopped immediately in the same round that aid of any kind is administered. Even after returning to 1+ hp via healing, the character will remain in a coma for **1d6 turns** and must rest for a minimum of one week before they can resume any sort of strenuous activity, mental or physical. If reduced to **-6 hp or below**, the wound's scars likely mark the character for life.

Slain characters can be raised via [[Raise Dead\|Raise Dead]] or [[Resurrection\|Resurrection]] (with exceptions: elves and half-orcs have spirits rather than souls, and cannot be raised or resurrected). NPC High Priests typically charge at least 1,000 gp for the casting.

## Key Facts

- **0 hp:** unconscious, bleeding 1 hp/round
- **-10 hp:** dead
- **Aid in same round:** stops the bleeding immediately
- **After waking:** 1d6 turns coma + 1 week of rest minimum
- **-6 hp or worse:** lasting scars
- **Raise Dead/Resurrection:** see [[Resurrection Success\|Resurrection Success]]
- **Poison-caused death:** see [[Poison\|Poison]] — may be reversed if neutralised in time
- **Elves/half-orcs:** unaffected by [[Raise Dead\|Raise Dead]] or [[Resurrection\|Resurrection]] — their spirits are not souls
