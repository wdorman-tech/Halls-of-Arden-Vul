## Notes

The vanilla [[OSRIC\|OSRIC]] economy is broken in a particular way: gold-for-XP plus mandatory training-cost gold sink creates a vicious cycle that destroys the in-fiction economy. This houserule rewrites both ends.

## The Rule

**Gold no longer awards XP.** Bringing gold out of the dungeon is no longer a direct XP source. (You can still spend it for XP via downtime — see [[Downtime\|Downtime]].)

**Training is no longer required.** Once you have the XP for your next level and rest in a safe location (out of the dungeon, or at least in a friendly faction's base), you level up immediately. No mandatory training cost.

**Exploration awards XP.** Completing **[[Tasks (XP)\|Tasks]]** during play earns the party percentages of the party's TXP (total XP needed for everyone to level once). See [[Exploration Awards\|Exploration Awards]] for the full mechanic.

**Downtime can spend gold for XP** at varying rates — see [[Downtime\|Downtime]] and the four activities ([[Training (Downtime)\|Training (Downtime)]], [[Connecting (Downtime)\|Connecting (Downtime)]], [[Carousing\|Carousing]], [[Recuperating (Downtime)\|Recuperating (Downtime)]]).

## Why this matters

- Players are rewarded for **exploration and creativity**, not just monster-slaying or treasure-hauling.
- The economy is no longer destabilised by ever-larger gold-for-XP transfers.
- Training-cost gold sink is gone, so the gold the players earn stays in their pockets — until they choose to spend it on downtime, training NPCs, or anything else.
- "Get to a safe place to level up" is a soft pacing mechanic that motivates returning to base.
