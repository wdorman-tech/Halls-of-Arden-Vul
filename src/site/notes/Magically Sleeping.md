## Notes

A **magically-sleeping** character or monster cannot move or take any actions. They are **physically helpless**.

If you're holding a hand weapon and are not in melee range of another enemy, you can **automatically hit and kill** the magically-sleeping creature with your weapon, provided your weapon can affect it.

When another enemy is in melee range of you: weapon attacks, unarmed attacks, or touch spells **always hit** the magically-sleeping target (unless you choose to miss). On a hit, you may choose to inflict **maximum damage** instead of rolling. **If every target is magically sleeping, unconscious, or held, your attacks per round are doubled.**

## Key Facts

- **Effects:** helpless; no actions
- **No threat near:** automatic kill (if weapon can affect)
- **Threat near:** automatic hit, max damage option, double attacks vs. all-helpless targets
- **Save:** typically vs. spells on first effect (some don't allow saves; e.g. [[Sleep\|Sleep]] HD-based)
- **Duration:** spell-dependent (typically minutes to hours)
- **Removal:** [[Dispel Magic\|Dispel Magic]], rough handling/loud noise/etc. — **but not damage** unless source allows
- **Common sources:** [[Sleep\|Sleep]] spell, [[Symbol\|Symbol]], certain monster songs (e.g. [[Harpy\|Harpy]])
- **Distinct from:** [[Unconscious\|Unconscious]] (knocked out by hp loss); [[Sleeping (Normal Sleep)\|Sleeping (Normal Sleep)]]
