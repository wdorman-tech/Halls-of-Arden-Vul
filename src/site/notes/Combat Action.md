## Notes

A **combat action** is what a character does during a [[Combat Round\|round]]. Combat actions are normally **declared by players before [[Initiative\|initiative]] is rolled**, and the GM forms an idea of the monsters' actions before declaring as well. Once declared, a character cannot change their action unless the original plan becomes impossible.

Common combat actions each have specific rules. Anything not covered by a rule is determined by the GM via common sense.

## Key Facts

**Common combat actions:**
- [[Charge\|Charge]] (move + attack at 2x MR, +2 to hit)
- **Closing into combat** (move up to full MR, no attack that round; opponent also can't attack until next round)
- [[Fleeing from Combat\|Fleeing from Combat]] (provokes a +4 attack from melee opponents)
- [[Hold Initiative\|Hold Initiative]] (delay, but must act by segment 6)
- [[Melee Attack\|Melee Attack]]
- [[Missile Attack\|Missile Attack]]
- [[Set Weapon Against Charge\|Set Weapon Against Charge]]
- [[Spellcasting\|Spell]] (declared with the spell name)
- [[Parrying\|Parrying]]
- **Negotiation/diplomacy** (Reaction Roll table)
- [[Unarmed Combat\|Unarmed Combat]] (Brawling, Grappling, Overbearing)
- **Other** (drink potion, climb wall, pull lever, etc.) — GM adjudicates

- **Contingencies allowed:** "I parry if charged, otherwise flee"
- **GM flexibility:** if declared action becomes impossible, GM allows the closest substitute
- **Spells are special:** if the spell is no longer castable, the slot is still lost (no substitute)
