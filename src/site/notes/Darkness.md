## Spell Stats

- **Class:** [[Illusionist\|Illusionist]] / [[Cleric\|Cleric]]
- **Level:** 1st (Illusionist) / 1st (Cleric)
- **School:** [[Phantasmal Transmutation\|Phantasmal Transmutation]] / [[Divine Transmutation\|Divine Transmutation]]
- **Range:** 40ft + 10ft per illusionist level / 60ft (Cleric)
- **Duration:** 2d4 rounds + 1 round per illusionist level / 3 turns + 1 turn per two cleric levels
- **Area of Effect:** 15ft radius globe (Illusionist) / 20ft radius sphere (Cleric)
- **Components:** V, S
- **Casting Time:** Fast (Illusionist) / 4 segments (Cleric)
- **Saving Throw:** None (Illusionist) / See below (Cleric)

## Notes

The caster creates magical darkness, centred on a target location, which can be a creature, object, or even a location in thin air. The caster can eliminate the darkness at will. Light sources, including magical light such as from a glowing sword, do not illuminate the area affected by the spell, but an actual [[Light\|Light]] spell may counteract this spell. For the Illusionist version, [[Infravision\|Infravision]] does not work in the affected area.

## Details

If the target is a light source, then the light is extinguished for the spell's duration but no additional magical darkness is created. The light source is simply cancelled.

The darkness is stationary, unless cast on a creature or object that moves (the caster cannot order the spell effect to move after casting).

If the target is a creature, then it can choose to roll a [[Saving Throw\|Saving Throw]] vs spells. The target creature applies its Dexterity save modifier, if it has one, to this saving throw. If it passes the saving throw, then the spell produces a stationary point of darkness in the air just behind the target creature, rather than affecting the creature itself.

As an optional rule, some GMs rule that infravision can see through the divine version of Darkness spells. This optional rule only affects Darkness spells cast using a cleric spell slot.

## Reversed Form

The cleric version is the reverse of [[Light\|Light]].
