## Notes

After [[Surprise\|Surprise]] segments are resolved and actions are declared, the first [[Combat Round\|combat round]] begins. At the start of each round, each side rolls **initiative** on a d6. The roll represents the **six-second [[Combat Segment\|segment]]** of the round in which the entire group will be able to act — **a low roll is better** because the group acts earlier.

Example: monsters roll a 6 and the party rolls a 1. The party acts in segment 1; the monsters act in segment 6.

[[Fighter\|Fighters]], [[Ranger\|rangers]], and [[Paladin\|paladins]] with multiple attacks make those attacks in specific segments (typically 1 and 10), regardless of the group initiative. Missile attackers may attack earlier in the round if they have a [[Dexterity\|Dexterity]]-based missile bonus.

## Key Facts

- **Roll:** d6 per side, low is best
- **Range:** 1-6 (segments 7-10 are reserved for delayed/multi-attack/spell effects)
- **Dexterity missile bonus:** subtracted from the segment number (earlier action) for missile-armed characters who declared an attack
- **Multi-attack fighter-types:** segments 1 and 10
- **Tie:** simultaneous resolution — both sides act at the same time, all attacks resolve, exception is [[Weapon Speed Factor\|Weapon Speed Factor]]
- **Hold initiative:** see [[Hold Initiative\|Hold Initiative]]
- **You can delay** until segment 6, but lose the action if you don't act by then
- **Why d6 not d10:** the round is 10 segments; the last 4 segments handle delayed effects and slow spells
