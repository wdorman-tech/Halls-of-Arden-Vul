## Notes

For convenience and tradition, **enclosed locations** are collectively called "dungeons" — not just made-made tunnels but any indoor adventure location: castle dungeons, natural caves, abandoned mines, sewers, fortresses, wizard's towers, shrines.

Dungeon adventures are resolved in **turns** (10 minutes each). While exploring, each turn is resolved separately (more experienced GMs run them together).

## Key Facts

**Order of operations per turn:**
1. **[[Wandering Monster\|Wandering Monster]] check** — every 3rd turn (1 in 6 standard); if hit, jump to step 3
2. **Statement of action** — players describe what they're doing:
   - **Move:** full MR/turn cautious; 5x for fast (no mapping, familiar areas only)
   - **Listen:** 1 round, 10% (15% for elves/gnomes/halflings/half-orcs); max 3 tries per door
   - **Open stuck/locked door:** 1 round per attempt
   - **Visual room survey:** 1 segment from open doorway with light
   - **Quick room check:** 1 turn per 20x20 ft area
   - **Search for traps:** 1 round per object (thieves/assassins only)
   - **Thoroughly search for secret doors:** 1 turn per 10x10 ft area
   - **Cast a spell:** see spell description
   - **[[Rest\|Rest]]:** 1 turn in 6, plus 1 after combat
   - **Other:** GM adjudicates
3. **Encounters** — surprise, distance, reactions, then combat (Chapter Six)
4. **Book-keeping** — record turn elapsed; deduct lost hp, expiring spells, burning torches

**Optional party caller:** one player speaks for the group (consult-then-report, not "leader")
**[[Marching Order\|Marching Order]]:** front/middle/rear declared, especially on entry
**[[Light Sources\|Light Sources]]:** essential unless all party have infravision
**Mapping:** key but easy to overdo — player draws a "trailing map" to track explored areas
**Levels of dungeon:** depth = danger and treasure scaling
