## Notes

Many spells, traps, and special attacks by monsters allow a character to make a **saving throw** to avoid or reduce the effect. A saving throw is rolled on a d20. The "target number" depends on the character's [[Class\|Class]] and [[Level\|Level]] (each class has its own saving throw table) and on the nature of the threat (such as "aimed magic items," "breath weapon," "paralysis," "poison," "spells," etc.). If the d20 roll meets or exceeds the target number, the saving throw succeeds.

Some saves are "all-or-nothing" (a successful save means no effect, e.g. against most poisons), while others are for the "lesser of two evils" (a successful save reduces an effect, e.g. halving the damage from a [[Fireball\|Fireball]]).

A roll of natural 1 always fails regardless of modifiers.

## Key Facts

- **Roll:** d20, must equal or exceed target number
- **Categories:** Aimed Magic Items / Breath Weapon / Paralysis & Poison / Petrifaction & Polymorph / Spells (and Rods/Staves/Wands)
- **Modifiers:** [[Wisdom\|Wisdom]] (mental saves), [[Dexterity\|Dexterity]] (agility), magical items, [[Cover\|Cover]] (+2 vs. dexterity-based saves with 25%+ cover)
- **Item saves:** see [[Item Saving Throw\|Item Saving Throw]] when gear may be damaged
- **Class tables:** each class has its own progression — see [[Cleric\|Cleric]], [[Fighter\|Fighter]], [[Magic-User\|Magic-User]], [[Thief\|Thief]], etc.
