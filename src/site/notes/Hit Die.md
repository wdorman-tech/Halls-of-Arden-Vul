## Notes

A **hit die** (HD) is the die rolled to determine how many [[Hit Points\|hit points]] a character or monster gains. Each [[Class\|Class]] uses a particular die size (for example, fighters use d10, magic-users use d4). When a character gains a level, they roll their class hit die and add the result (modified by [[Constitution\|Constitution]]) to their total hit points, until they reach the level cap for rolling — after that, most classes gain a fixed bonus per level (e.g. magic-users get +1 hp per level after 11).

For monsters, "hit dice" measure relative power and are usually d8s. A 4 HD monster rolls 4d8 for hit points.

## Key Facts

- **Roll once per level:** when [[Gaining Levels\|Gaining Levels]]
- **Constitution:** adjustment applies on each roll, but minimum gain is +1 hp
- **Constitution 19:** any roll of "1" is treated as a "2"
- **Fixed bonus tier:** post-cap, no [[Constitution\|Constitution]] adjustment is applied
- **Monsters:** typically d8 per HD; modifies Turning Undead progression too
