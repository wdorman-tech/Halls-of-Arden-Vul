## Notes

A weapon may be used to **beat down** rather than kill an opponent. When the player declares their attack as a subdual attack, the damage rolled is split: half is "real" damage and half is **subdual damage**, which does not kill.

A creature reduced to 0 hp or below *solely* by subdual damage is unconscious but not killed and is not bleeding out. However, if the total real damage equals or exceeds the creature's hp, the creature is treated normally — the blows were strong enough to potentially kill.

## Key Facts

- **Split:** half real, half subdual on each attack so designated
- **Recovery:** 1 hp per **hour**
- **Knocked out, not killed:** creature is unconscious but stable if real damage hasn't reached threshold
- **Not allowed in unarmed combat:** unarmed combat uses [[Damage\|Temporary Damage]] instead
- **Not all monsters can be subdued:** generally only humanoids and special creatures (e.g. [[Dragon\|Dragon]]). GM adjudicates.
- **Knightly tourneys:** subdual is sometimes negotiated in advance
