## Notes

[[Backstab\|Backstab]] is the signature combat ability of the [[Thief\|Thief]] (and [[Assassin\|Assassin]]). When the character is immediately behind a creature that isn't aware of them, they may strike with a melee weapon a single-classed thief can use, gaining a much improved chance to hit and a multiplier to weapon damage that scales with level.

## Key Facts

- **Class:** [[Thief\|Thief]] and [[Assassin\|Assassin]]
- **Conditions:** Must be immediately behind the target; target must be unaware of the attacker; weapon must be one a single-classed thief can use
- **To-Hit Bonus:** +4 to hit (replaces the usual +2 rear-attack bonus)
- **Damage Multiplier (or extra dice — statistically identical):**
    - Levels 1-4: x2 (or +1 die)
    - Levels 5-8: x3 (or +2 dice)
    - Levels 9-12 (Thief) / 9-12 (Assassin): x4 (or +3 dice)
    - Levels 13-16 (Thief): x5 (or +4 dice)
    - Levels 17-20 (Thief): x6 (or +5 dice)
- **Damage Calculation:** Multiply the weapon die roll BEFORE adding bonuses (Strength, magic, etc.). If another multiplier could apply (e.g. magic weapon), use only one — your choice.
- **Combat Backstab (Recommended Rule):** If combat is already audible, the thief only needs a successful [[Hide in Shadows\|Hide in Shadows]] roll to set up a backstab; the noise of combat covers their approach. (Without this rule, both [[Move Silently\|Move Silently]] and [[Hide in Shadows\|Hide in Shadows]] are needed, making low-level backstabs nearly impossible.)
- **Failed Assassin Assassination:** If an [[Assassin\|Assassin]]'s [[Assassinate\|assassination]] attempt fails, the target still takes weapon damage as if normally hit, but the failed attempt does NOT count as a backstab.
