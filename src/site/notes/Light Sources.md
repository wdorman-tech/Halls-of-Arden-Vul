## Notes

In a dungeon, adventurers need a [[Light Sources\|light source]] unless every character has [[Light and Vision\|infravision]]. Torches can be blown out by wind, water, or magic, so lanterns or backup illumination are usually a good idea.

## Key Facts

| Source | Illumination | Duration / Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Bullseye lantern** | 80 ft, 10 ft wide beam | 1 pint oil / 4 hours; can be hooded (dark) |
| **Hooded lantern** | 30 ft radius | 1 pint oil / 4 hours; can be hooded |
| **Magical weapons** | dagger 10 ft / longsword 20 ft | Infinite; usually shed light when drawn; cannot be covered |
| **Torch** | 40 ft radius | 6 turns (1 hour); cannot be covered without extinguishing |
| **Standard candle** | 20 ft radius | 30 minutes; blows out easily |

- **Visibility from afar:** light sources are seen far past their illumination radius — approaching torchlight warns intelligent creatures
- **Special cases:** magic lamps and items have GM-determined ranges
- **Surprise:** a visible party light can change the [[Surprise\|Surprise]] check
