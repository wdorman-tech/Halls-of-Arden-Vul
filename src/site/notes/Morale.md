## Notes

**Morale** governs whether monsters and NPCs will continue to fight a losing battle. Player characters do not check morale, but [[Hireling\|hirelings]], [[Henchman\|henchmen]], and [[Men-at-arms\|men-at-arms]] should be checked normally.

The base morale for most monsters is **50% + 5% per [[Hit Die\|Hit Die]]**. Roll **d100** — if the result is **higher than** the modified morale number, the check **fails** and the creature seeks to retreat, surrender, or flee.

**Some creatures never check morale:** trolls, most undead, non-intelligent creatures, and other "fearless" types.

## Key Facts

- **Base:** 50% + 5% per HD (so 8+1 HD = 50 + 40 = 90% base)
- **Roll:** d100, must be ≤ base to pass
- **When to check:** when monsters are clearly losing or taking unacceptable losses
- **Maximum 2 checks per battle:** if they pass twice, they fight to the death
- **Modifiers (cumulative):**
  - +5% per friend killed/surrendered/fled
  - +5% own side at 25% casualties
  - +10% numerical inferiority
  - +15% own side at 50% casualties
  - +20% greatly outnumbered (2:1+)
  - +25% own leader defeated/downed
  - -5% per foe killed/surrendered/fled
  - -5% own side inflicted 25% casualties
  - -10% numerical superiority
  - -15% own side inflicted 50% casualties
- **NPC modifiers:** [[Charisma\|Charisma]] of leader, loyalty modifiers (Section 1.7.4)
- **Failure result:**
  - by ≤ 25%: retreat
  - by ≥ 51%: surrender (unless circumstances dictate fight or flight)
- **Never surrenders:** any creature that has seen execution-of-prisoners evidence
