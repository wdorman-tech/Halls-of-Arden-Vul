## Spell Stats

- **Class:** [[Illusionist\|Illusionist]]
- **Level:** 1st
- **School:** [[Phantasmal Enchantment\|Phantasmal Enchantment]]
- **Range:** 30ft
- **Duration:** 1 round + 1 round per illusionist level
- **Area of Effect:** 1d6 creatures
- **Components:** V, S
- **Casting Time:** Fast
- **Saving Throw:** Negates effect

## Notes

The gestures of this spell weave a hypnotic power into the caster's words, affecting 1d6 creatures with a [[Suggestion\|Suggestion]] the caster makes.

## Details

Those not making their [[Saving Throw\|Saving Throw]]s will believe a suggestion made by the caster. The only indication about whether a creature has been affected by the hypnotism is whether or not it responds to the suggestion.

The caster can make factual suggestions ("These are not the halflings you seek"), suggestions of a course of action ("Don't mention you saw us"), or a combination of both.

Even a victim who has failed a saving throw against the spell will not undertake a course of action that is unreasonable, but the experienced caster can easily phrase requests in such a way as to avoid this problem. Example: No creature will leap off a cliff for no reason; but the caster might "need to scout the bottom of a chasm" and promise to cast a [[Feather Fall\|Feather Fall]] spell, or hand the victim a "magical" torch that supposedly permits the power of flight.

The creature to be influenced must be able to hear and understand the language spoken by the caster.
