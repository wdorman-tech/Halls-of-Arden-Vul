## Notes

**Resting** is required during exploration to keep characters from becoming fatigued. While resting, the party cannot move or perform any strenuous action (passive activities such as mapping are allowed).

In a dungeon, characters must rest for one [[Combat Round#Turn\|turn]] out of every six, plus one turn after each combat, plus a double-length (two turns) rest after an evasion or pursuit. For longer expeditions, the party may camp inside the dungeon for a "night" to recover spells.

Rest is also the trigger for [[Natural Healing\|Natural Healing]] and for memorising spells (an uninterrupted four-hour rest in a quiet place is required before [[Spellcasting\|memorising]]).

## Key Facts

- **Standard dungeon rest:** 1 turn (10 minutes) every 6 turns
- **After combat:** 1 turn rest
- **After pursuit/escape:** 2 turn rest
- **Spell memorisation:** 4 hours uninterrupted rest, then 15 minutes per spell level
- **Skipping rest:** GM-adjudicated fatigue: reduced movement, combat penalties, lower ability scores, lower NPC morale
- **Natural healing:** 1 hp per day of uninterrupted rest
