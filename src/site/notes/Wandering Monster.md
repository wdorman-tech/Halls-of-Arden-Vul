## Notes

A **wandering monster** is a randomly-encountered creature that may appear during dungeon exploration. The standard wandering monster check is made **once every 3 turns**, with a **1 in 6** chance of an encounter. Specific dungeons may use different parameters.

The check serves two purposes:
1. **Verisimilitude:** creates a "living dungeon" feel, with monsters patrolling, foraging, or exploring
2. **Pacing:** discourages players from wasting time methodically searching every inch — without a time pressure, play would slow to a crawl

## Key Facts

- **Standard check:** every 3 turns
- **Standard chance:** 1 in 6 per check
- **Tables:** found in the GM Guide or specific module
- **Adjusted by:** stealth, speed, party noise, location (heavily-trafficked areas worse)
- **Loud activity** (chopping doors, multiple stuck-door attempts) increases the chance
- **Stealthy parties** should encounter fewer
- **Levels of difficulty:** wandering monsters scale with [[Dungeon Adventure\|dungeon level]] — deeper levels have stronger encounters
- **Reaction:** roll on Reaction table — not all wandering monsters are hostile (a "monster" is just any creature)
