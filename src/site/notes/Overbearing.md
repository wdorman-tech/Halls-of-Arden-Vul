## Notes

**Overbearing** is one of three [[Unarmed Combat\|Unarmed Combat]] types (alongside Brawling and [[Grappling\|Grappling]]). It's the tackling/knock-down attack: a horde of orcs is more likely to bring down a small force of high-level characters by overbearing them than by trading swordstrokes.

Unlike grappling, **overbearing can be done with weapon and shield in hand** — it doesn't require both hands free.

After fend-off, the attacker rolls the Unarmed To-Hit Roll. On a hit, roll **1d6** on the Overbearing Attack Results table.

## Key Facts

**Overbearing Results (1d6 + modifiers):**
| Roll | Result | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| 1 or lower | Total Failure | Attacker falls prone; target unaffected |
| 2 | Partial Failure | Attacker prone, clutching feet; target's MR reduced by 30, no DEX bonus; attacker is grappling, defender is not |
| 3-4 | Partial Success | Target [[Staggered\|Staggered]] and [[Overborne\|Overborne]], not prone; attacker gets immediate follow-up move (melee, brawl, or grapple roll) |
| 5-6 | Success | Target prone and overborne; attacker gets immediate follow-up; damage = die roll (1 real, rest temporary) |
| 7+ | Total Success | Target prone, overborne, may not act next round; immediate follow-up at +2 (max grapple result 8); damage = die roll (2 real, rest temporary) |

**Modifiers:**
- **Attacker STR 16-18 / 4-8 HD:** +1
- **Attacker STR 18.01+ / 9+ HD:** +2
- **Size:** -4 Tiny, -2 Small, +0 Medium, +2 Large, +6 Huge, +10 Gargantuan
- **Four legs used for motion:** +2 (attacker only)
- **Defender:** mirror of attacker modifiers
- **Four-legged defender:** **cannot be overborne**

**Strength damage modifiers:** added to damage but **not** to the die roll.
