## Notes

A [[Thieves' Guild\|Thieves' Guild]] is the [[Thief\|Thief]]'s name-level institution. At 10th level a thief becomes famous enough to take over an existing guild or establish their own. The guild typically consists of 6d4 NPC thieves of varying levels — and to take over an existing one, the leader must be displaced or removed.

## Key Facts

- **Class:** [[Thief\|Thief]]
- **Level Required:** 10th (Guildmaster)
- **Composition:** 6d4 NPC thieves of varying levels
- **Establishment:** Either take over an existing guild (displace/remove its leader) or establish a new one
- **Stronghold:** May be combined with a stronghold in a town or city
- **Read Scrolls (concurrent ability):** At level 10 the thief gains a chance to cast spells from arcane, phantasmal, or druidic scrolls — roll 1d4; on 1 the casting fails (scroll survives); otherwise the spell is cast but with a 5% chance of being reversed or harmful, with the scroll erased.
