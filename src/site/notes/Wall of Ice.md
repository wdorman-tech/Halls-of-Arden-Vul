## Spell Stats

- **Class:** [[Magic-User\|Magic-User]]
- **Level:** 4th
- **School:** [[Arcane Evocation\|Arcane Evocation]]
- **Range:** 10ft per MU level
- **Duration:** 1 turn per MU level
- **Area of Effect:** Wall, area 100 square ft per level, 10ft thick per level
- **Components:** V, S, M
- **Casting Time:** 4 segments
- **Saving Throw:** None

## Notes

A wall of ice spell creates a huge wall of ice, with a front face of up to 100 square ft per level. Its exact dimensions are adjustable by the caster.

## Details

Example Dimensions: If the wall is cast by a 20th level caster, the dimensions of the wall could be any combination of dimensions that multiply to equal 2,000 squareft. The wall might be 10ft high and 200ft long, or 5ft high and 400ft long, or 20ft high and 100ft long, etc. The thickness of the wall cannot be affected by the caster; it is always 10ft.

The wall need not be cast so that it is attached to anything, and it can even be cast in the air to fall upon opponents, doing 3d10 points of damage in the area where it falls.

The wall cannot be used to "entomb" an opponent; it pushes people aside as it forms.

Breaking through the ice with a melee weapon causes 2 hit points of damage from ice shards per inch of thickness, and can be broken out at a rate of 10ft per round.

Normal fire has no measurable effect upon the wall, but magical fire (and dragon fire) will melt through it in one round (causing a massive cloud of water vapour to form and linger for a full turn, obscuring vision by 50%).
