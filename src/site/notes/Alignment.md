## Notes

A typical creature in the game world has an alignment, which broadly describes its moral and personal attitudes. Alignments are linked to powerful supernatural forces in the multiverse — these forces are self-aware and can communicate with gods and mortals, but they are so alien that their messages are usually misunderstood and sometimes drive their hearers mad.

Alignment runs along two axes: [[Law and Chaos\|Law and Chaos]] (the cosmic axis) and [[Good and Evil\|Good and Evil]] (the moral axis). The intersection of the two yields nine alignments. For most creatures alignment is a choice and may shift over time; for some — most notably outsiders like demons — alignment is essential to their nature and cannot change.

## Houserule

> **From the Player Doc:** "I am removing alignment restrictions from classes, although clerics still have to match the alignment of their deity."

This means a [[Paladin\|Paladin]] does not have to be Lawful Good, a [[Druid\|Druid]] does not have to be True Neutral, etc. The one exception is the [[Cleric\|Cleric]]: a cleric's alignment must match the alignment of the deity they serve (see [[The Ten\|The Ten]] and [[The Seventeen\|The Seventeen]] for deity alignments). This trades some genre-policing for player freedom while preserving the religious logic of the cleric class.

## Key Facts

- **Two axes:** [[Law and Chaos\|Law and Chaos]] (cosmic), [[Good and Evil\|Good and Evil]] (moral)
- **Nine alignments:** [[Lawful Good\|Lawful Good]], [[Neutral Good\|Neutral Good]], [[Chaotic Good\|Chaotic Good]], [[Lawful Neutral\|Lawful Neutral]], [[True Neutral\|True Neutral]], [[Chaotic Neutral\|Chaotic Neutral]], [[Lawful Evil\|Lawful Evil]], [[Neutral Evil\|Neutral Evil]], [[Chaotic Evil\|Chaotic Evil]]
- **Each alignment has its own tongue:** see [[Alignment Language\|Alignment Language]]
- **Class restrictions removed in this campaign** (except: clerics must match their deity's alignment)
- **Some creatures have inherent alignment** — demons are embodiments of Chaotic Evil and cannot shift
