## Notes

A **combat segment** is a six-second unit of time, with **ten segments per [[Combat Round\|round]]**. Segments are used to time individual actions within a round: when [[Initiative\|initiative]] is rolled, the segment number on the d6 is the segment in which that side first gets to act.

Spells with a casting time take effect on (initiative segment + casting time - 1). Multi-attack fighter-types attack in specific segments (typically 1 and 10). Movement, if tracked segment by segment, proceeds at 1/10 normal round movement per segment.

## Key Facts

- **Length:** 6 seconds
- **Per round:** 10 segments
- **Initiative range:** segments 1-6 (later segments reserved for ongoing effects)
- **Spell timing:** segment of casting + casting time − 1 = segment spell takes effect
- **Spillover:** spells that would resolve past segment 10 happen in next round (e.g. cast in 6 with 8-segment casting → segment 13 → segment 3 of next round)
- **Movement per segment:** 1/10 of round movement
- **Surprise segments:** during surprise, only the unsurprised side acts; full actions can still occur in a single surprise segment
