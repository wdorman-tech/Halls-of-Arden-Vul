## Notes

Encumbrance in base [[OSRIC\|OSRIC]] tracks pounds of carried gear and is fiddly. This houserule replaces it with a slot-based system inspired by video-game inventories.

## The Rule

Each character has a grid of slots that they place items on. Each row of six slots has a [[Strength\|Strength]] score associated with it; placing items in rows that your Strength does not reach incurs encumbrance penalties:

| Extra rows beyond Strength | Effect |
|---|---|
| 1 | Movement to ¾ base |
| 2 | Movement halved; no surprise bonuses |
| 3 | Movement to ¼; no surprise bonuses; -1 surprise |
| 4 | Cannot move; no surprise bonuses; -2 surprise |

**Stacking:**
- Coins: stack up to 50 in a slot
- Small items (torches, rations): stack up to 4
- Large items (claymore, medium+ shield): 2 adjacent slots
- Very large/unwieldy (tent, cooking pot): 3 slots in a line
- Some treasure (jewelry): does **not** take slots

**Armor:**
- Reduces movement to 90ft (banded, padded, studded, ring, chain): 4 slots in a 2x2 square
- Reduces movement to 60ft (splint, scale, plate): 9 slots in a 3x3 square
- No movement reduction (leather, elfin mail): 1 slot
- Non-AC clothing: no slots

**Containers:** do not take slots themselves; they encompass slots that other items go in. Items must be in a container unless worn or held.

| Container | Slots | Special |
|---|---|---|
| Backpack | 9 (3x3) | None; every PC starts with one free |
| Quiver | 1-2 | Ammunition stacks up to 12 |
| Flask | 1 | Liquids stack up to 3 |
| Writing Satchel | 1 | One book + 10 parchment + writing tools (or up to 2 scrolls in place of parchment) |
| Pouch / Purse | 1 or 4 (2x2) | None |
| Sack | 4 (2x2) or 9 (3x3) | None |
| Scroll Case | 1 | Up to 3 scrolls |

## Why this matters

This replaces the lb-based encumbrance check with a visual, gridded one. Strength still gates capacity (each row is keyed to a Strength score), so [[Strength\|Strength]] remains the relevant attribute, but tracking is much faster at the table.
