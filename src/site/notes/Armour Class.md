## Concept

Armour Class is a measure of how difficult it is to damage you with a sword or other weapon. It is one of the two important numbers on the "To-Hit" tables in the combat section.

## Descending vs. Ascending AC

OSRIC supports two systems and your group must choose which to use.

### Descending AC (Default)

In the original AD&D system, the **lower** your AC, the better — a lower AC was harder to hit. This is the default for OSRIC.

- Unarmoured = AC 10
- [[Plate Mail\|Plate Mail]] = AC 3
- [[Plate Mail\|Plate Mail]] +1 with shield +1 = AC 0

### Ascending AC (Optional)

Modern game systems use an **higher-is-better** Ascending AC. OSRIC uses bracketed numbers to support this; the descending and ascending values always add up to **20**.

- AC 4 [16] -> if using Ascending, your AC is **16**.

If your group uses Ascending AC, you can use a formula in which the AC number IS the target number to hit. You make attack rolls with a **Base To Hit Bonus (BTHB)** added to the d20; if the result equals or exceeds the opponent's [AC], the attack hits.

**Special Rule (Ascending only):** A natural 20 gains an automatic +5 to the die roll. Do **not** add this bonus when using the regular descending attack tables.

## Magical Armour & Shields

If you wear magical armour and hold a magical shield, you get **both** magical bonuses.

Example: +1 [[Plate Mail\|Plate Mail]] (AC 3 [17] base, becomes AC 2 [18]) plus a +1 [[Shield, Small\|shield]] (-1 [+1] base, becomes -2 [+2]) gives you an armour class of **0 [20]**.

## Shield Rules

Shields improve your AC by 1 (an unarmoured person with a shield is AC 9 [11]). You can only use a shield if your attacker is in front of you or attacking from your shield side, and you can't use a shield against an attacker you aren't aware of.

| Shield | Attacks Blocked per Round |
|---|---|
| [[Shield, Small\|Shield, Small]] | 1 |
| [[Shield, Medium\|Shield, Medium]] | 2 |
| [[Shield, Large\|Shield, Large]] | 3 |

Decide whether to use the shield bonus just before the attacker rolls.
