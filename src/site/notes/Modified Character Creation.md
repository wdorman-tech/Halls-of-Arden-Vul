## Notes

Several houserules adjust character creation to make starting characters fragile but viable, and to commit the campaign to a single mode of generation.

## The Rule

**Ability Scores:** Roll **3d6 for each score in order**. After rolling, you may **switch any two scores**. (This is a slight softening of OSRIC's "Hardest Mode" — see [[Ability Score\|Ability Score]] generation modes.)

**Ancestry:**
- No maximum levels — pick what you love and progress without ceiling.
- Humans get **maximum value on first-level hit points** to compensate for losing the demihuman edge from removed level limits.
- See [[Ancestry Houserules\|Ancestry Houserules]] for the gnome / Imperial Goblin / Common rules.

**Class:**
- Alignment restrictions removed (see [[Alignment\|Alignment]] houserule); clerics still must match deity alignment.
- No multiclassing or dual classing — see [[Class Houserules\|Class Houserules]].
- Magic-Users automatically know **Read Magic** in addition to two random and two of-choice spells.
- Magic-Users must choose one of the [[Seven Collegia\|Seven Collegia]].

**Equipment:** Same gear list, but encumbrance is by [[Slot-Based Encumbrance\|Slot-Based Encumbrance]].

**Extra (Rumours):** Every starting character gets **1d3 [[Adventuring Rumour\|adventuring rumours]]** and **1d2 [[Historical Rumour\|historical rumours]]**. Each contains at least some truth, but accuracy is not guaranteed. Backup characters and post-start characters do **not** get rumours.

## Why this matters

- 3d6-in-order with one swap keeps stats in the OSRIC-canonical low range while granting a tiny amount of agency.
- Removing level caps and giving humans max-HP-at-1 rebalances the ancestries upward together.
- Free Read Magic and a forced collegium choice make every magic-user PC a participant in the empire's regulated magical politics.
- Starting rumours seed every character into the world's mysteries from session 1.
