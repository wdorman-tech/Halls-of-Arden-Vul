## Spell Stats

- **Class:** [[Cleric\|Cleric]] / [[Magic-User\|Magic-User]]
- **Level:** 1st (Cleric) / 2nd (Magic-User)
- **School:** [[Divine Divination\|Divine Divination]] / [[Arcane Divination\|Arcane Divination]]
- **Range:** Caster
- **Duration:** 1 turn, plus 5 rounds per cleric level / 5 rounds per MU level
- **Area of Effect:** Path 10ft wide and 120ft long (Cleric) / 60ft long (Magic-User)
- **Components:** V, S, M (Cleric) / V, S (Magic-User)
- **Casting Time:** 1 round (Cleric) / 2 segments (Magic-User)
- **Saving Throw:** None

## Notes

The caster sees an aura around each evil-aligned creature, object, or place within the area of effect. If the creature, object, or place is invisible, then the caster knows where it is (surrounding aura), but still can't see details of the actual invisible thing. If they make an attack roll against it, then the "to hit" roll penalty for attacking an invisible target is halved, so usually -2 instead of -4.

## Details

Only the caster "sees" the aura of evil presences. This spell reveals evil alignments, not danger, so won't necessarily spot a mindless trap or a poisoned wineglass. The spell doesn't necessarily detect curses, although some of the most atrocious curses do make the cursed item inherently evil.

Opaque materials can interfere with the spell. The spell can only penetrate metal if it's one inch thick or less, and brick or stone if it's one foot thick or less.

This spell only detects a creature, object, or place if it has an evil alignment or if something in the rules specifically describes it as evil, fiendish, demonic, or devilish. Otherwise, this spell doesn't detect it.

The caster must concentrate on maintaining the spell, or it ends. See [[Concentration\|Concentration]] for how this works.

## Reversed Form

The reverse is [[Detect Good\|Detect Good]].
